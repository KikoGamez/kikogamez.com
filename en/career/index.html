Professional Biography – Kiko Gámez

Kiko Gámez Martínez is an executive in the digital and technology sector with more than twenty years of experience leading projects in transformation, growth, and business management within multinational companies. His career combines a strong technical foundation in telecommunications engineering with strategic management skills and leadership of high-performance teams in highly competitive environments.

Education and Early Career

Kiko graduated as a Telecommunications Engineer in 2000. From the beginning of his career, he clearly understood the importance of combining technical knowledge with a global business vision. His professional journey began at Cisco Systems, where he participated in data network deployment projects across Spain and earned prestigious certifications such as CCDA and CCNA. Shortly after, he joined Motorola, working in development centers in Chicago, Vancouver, and the United Kingdom, with responsibilities in pioneering 3G network projects. Between 2001 and 2003, he led initiatives in countries such as China and Australia, being responsible for the first real-world implementation of a Motorola 3G network globally.

Transition into Management

Aware of the importance of expanding his profile beyond engineering, in 2004 Kiko completed an Executive MBA at IESE Business School, while continuing his role at Motorola as project manager for cable TV infrastructure across Europe. This period marked his transition towards management and business development. After a brief period at NEC as Network Sales Director, he joined Orange in 2007, where he led the mobile broadband division. In just a few years, he turned this business unit into a market leader in Spain, achieving a 38% market share.

Consolidation at Orange

Thanks to these results, he took over the Orange Care project, focused on differentiation through customer service excellence. Under his leadership, the churn rate was reduced by five percentage points, and new business lines were developed, generating more than €50 million in annual revenue. Later, Kiko was appointed Director of the SME and Self-Employed Segment, managing a portfolio of 1.4 million clients and €450 million. In this position, he successfully reduced churn from 22% to 16%, reinforcing customer loyalty and driving sustainable growth.

Period at Tuenti

In 2015, Kiko began a new chapter at Tuenti, Telefónica’s digital subsidiary. There, he led the telecommunications business, driving a 100% digital value proposition based on an innovative application. During this period, the company quadrupled its net customer acquisition in Spain and expanded operations to Argentina, Ecuador, Peru, and Mexico.

Leadership at Telefónica

In 2017, Telefónica invited him to lead the Digital Transformation of the Global Sales Channel, a strategic role in which he doubled the online channel’s contribution to the group’s total sales. Since 2022, he has held the position of Global Director of E-commerce, Martech & Growth at Telefónica, with responsibility for digital strategy, governance of the marketing technology stack, and the definition of data-driven growth models on a global scale.

Management Principles

Kiko’s professional career has been guided by a series of principles that define his way of working:

Business First

Technology is always a means to achieve results.

Clear Metrics

Every project must be measured to demonstrate its impact.

Simple Execution

Focus on a few priorities that generate high impact.

Leadership

Sustainable growth driven by talent, culture, and motivation.
